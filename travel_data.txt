Srinagar is the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, famous for its houseboats on Dal Lake, Mughal gardens, and vibrant local bazaars.
Dal Lake is a large lake in Srinagar known for its floating gardens, shikara rides, and stunning sunset views surrounded by the Zabarwan mountains.
Nishat Bagh is one of the largest Mughal gardens in Srinagar, located on the eastern shore of Dal Lake, featuring terraced lawns and fountains with panoramic lake views.
Shalimar Bagh is a Mughal garden in Srinagar built by Emperor Jahangir, renowned for its beautiful chinar trees, flowing water channels, and historical significance.
The Mughal Gardens of Srinagar include Nishat Bagh, Shalimar Bagh, and Chashme Shahi, all offering serene walks, beautiful floral displays, and views of Dal Lake.
Shankaracharya Temple is an ancient Hindu temple situated on a hilltop in Srinagar, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city and Dal Lake.
Hazratbal Shrine is a revered Muslim shrine located on the northern shore of Dal Lake, known for its white marble architecture and peaceful surroundings.
Lal Chowk is the commercial hub of Srinagar, famous for shopping, local handicrafts, Pashmina shawls, and traditional Kashmiri goods.
Gulmarg is a world-famous hill station located about 50 km from Srinagar, known for skiing, the Gulmarg Gondola (one of the highest cable cars in the world), and lush meadows.
Gulmarg Gondola is one of the highest cable cars in the world, offering spectacular views of the Himalayan peaks and access to Apharwat Peak for skiing and snowboarding.
In winter, Gulmarg transforms into India's premier ski destination with powder snow slopes suitable for both beginners and experienced skiers.
Gulmarg is also known for its beautiful golf course, one of the highest in the world, surrounded by pine and fir trees with stunning mountain backdrops.
Pahalgam is a scenic hill station located about 90 km from Srinagar, known as the Valley of Shepherds, famous for its lush green meadows, pine forests, and the Lidder River.
Betaab Valley in Pahalgam is named after the Bollywood movie Betaab, offering stunning views of snow-capped mountains, lush green meadows, and crystal clear streams.
Aru Valley near Pahalgam is a beautiful meadow perfect for camping, trekking, horse riding, and enjoying the peaceful Himalayan scenery.
Chandanwari is a popular destination near Pahalgam, serving as the starting point of the Amarnath Yatra, and features a natural snow bridge over the Lidder River.
Sonamarg, meaning Meadow of Gold, is located about 80 km from Srinagar and is known for its stunning glaciers, alpine lakes, and the gateway to the Zoji La pass.
Thajiwas Glacier in Sonamarg is a popular tourist spot where visitors can trek or ride ponies to reach the glacier, surrounded by stunning snow-covered peaks.
Sonamarg serves as the base camp for several treks including the famous Vishansar Lake trek and the Krishansar Lake trek.
Yusmarg is a peaceful meadow located 47 km from Srinagar, known for its scenic views of Pir Panjal mountain range, horse riding, and walking trails through dense forests.
Yusmarg is relatively untouched by mass tourism, making it an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility and undisturbed natural beauty.
Doodhpathri is an offbeat valley famous for its lush green pastures, milk-like flowing streams, pine forests, and is located about 42 km from Budgam.
Doodhpathri gets its name from the milky white streams that flow through the valley, and it offers stunning views of the Pir Panjal range with minimal crowds.
Gurez Valley is a remote Himalayan destination perfect for adventure lovers, known for its rugged mountain landscapes, wooden houses, and the Kishanganga River.
Gurez Valley is accessible only from May to November due to heavy snowfall that blocks Razdan Pass, making it one of the most pristine and untouched valleys in Kashmir.
The Habba Khatoon peak in Gurez Valley is named after a famous Kashmiri poetess and offers magnificent views, especially during sunrise and sunset.
Lolab Valley is a hidden gem in North Kashmir known for its dense forests, apple orchards, and the serene Wular Lake nearby, perfect for nature walks and photography.
Bangus Valley is a lesser-known valley in Kupwara district, surrounded by dense forests and snow-capped peaks, ideal for trekking, camping, and wildlife watching.
Sinthan Top is a high-altitude mountain pass at about 12,500 feet connecting Anantnag with Kishtwar, offering panoramic views of both the Kashmir Valley and the Chenab Valley.
Tarsar Marsar Trek is one of the most beautiful alpine lake treks in Kashmir, passing through meadows, pine forests, and leading to the twin lakes of Tarsar and Marsar.
The Great Lakes Trek in Kashmir is a 7-day trek covering multiple alpine lakes including Vishansar, Krishansar, Gadsar, Satsar, and Gangabal, considered one of the best treks in India.
Wular Lake is one of the largest freshwater lakes in Asia, located in Bandipora district, and offers boating, bird watching, and views of the surrounding mountains.
Manasbal Lake is often called the jewel of Kashmir, known for its lotus flower blooms in summer and excellent conditions for bird watching and kayaking.
Kokernag is a famous freshwater spring in Anantnag district, surrounded by a beautiful botanical garden with trout fish hatchery and walking paths.
Verinag is a historical spring at the foot of the Pir Panjal Range, believed to be the source of the Jhelum River, with Mughal-era octagonal tank and gardens.
Patnitop is a popular hill station in Udhampur district of Jammu region, known for its pine forests, panoramic views, paragliding, and snow activities in winter.
Vaishno Devi Temple in Katra is one of the most visited Hindu pilgrimage sites in India, nestled in the Trikuta Mountains at an altitude of 5,200 feet.
Bahu Fort in Jammu city is an ancient fort overlooking the Tawi River, housing a temple dedicated to Goddess Kali with beautiful gardens surrounding it.
Raghunath Temple in Jammu is one of the largest temple complexes in North India, featuring intricate architecture and housing a vast collection of ancient manuscripts.
Kashmiri Wazwan is a traditional multi-course meal served at weddings and special occasions, featuring dishes like Rogan Josh, Gushtaba, Yakhni, and Tabak Maaz.
Rogan Josh is a signature Kashmiri lamb curry cooked in a rich gravy of yogurt, fennel, and a blend of Kashmiri spices, known for its deep red color and aromatic flavors.
Kashmiri Kahwa is a traditional green tea infused with saffron, cardamom, cinnamon, and crushed almonds, perfect for warming up during cold Kashmir winters.
Noon Chai (also called Sheer Chai) is a pink-colored salted tea unique to Kashmir, traditionally served with Kashmiri bread (lavasa or girda) during breakfast.
The best time to visit Kashmir for flowers and green meadows is April to June when the tulip gardens bloom and the valleys are covered in wildflowers.
The best time for skiing and snow activities in Kashmir is December to February, particularly in Gulmarg which receives heavy snowfall.
September to November is ideal for visiting Kashmir to witness the autumn colors when the chinar trees turn golden and red, creating stunning landscapes.
For the Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage, the journey is typically open from late June to August, with the route going through Pahalgam or Baltal.
Srinagar can be reached by air through Sheikh ul-Alam International Airport, by road via the Jammu-Srinagar National Highway, or by train to Jammu followed by road travel.
A typical 5-day Kashmir itinerary includes Day 1 in Srinagar (Dal Lake, Mughal Gardens), Day 2 in Gulmarg (Gondola, skiing), Day 3 in Pahalgam (Betaab Valley, Aru Valley), Day 4 for Sonamarg (Thajiwas Glacier), and Day 5 shopping in Srinagar.
For a budget trip to Kashmir, consider staying in houseboats on Dal Lake, eating at local dhabas for authentic Kashmiri food, and using shared transport to tourist spots.
Pashmina shawls, walnut wood carvings, papier-mache items, Kashmiri carpets, saffron, and dried fruits are popular souvenirs to buy in Kashmir.
Pony rides are available at most tourist spots in Kashmir including Pahalgam, Gulmarg, Sonamarg, and Yusmarg, making it easy to explore areas not accessible by vehicle.
Shikara rides on Dal Lake are a must-do experience in Srinagar, offering views of floating markets, houseboats, and the Zabarwan mountains during golden hour.
The Mughal Road is an alternative scenic route connecting Poonch with Shopian, passing through dense forests, meadows, and the historic Pir Ki Gali pass.
Hemis National Park is a high-altitude national park in Ladakh famous for snow leopard sightings, Himalayan wildlife, and ancient Buddhist monasteries.
Dachigam National Park near Srinagar is home to the endangered Hangul (Kashmir stag) and offers scenic trails through dense forests and mountain landscapes.
River rafting is popular on the Lidder River in Pahalgam and on the Sindh River in Sonamarg, offering thrilling rapids amidst stunning valley scenery.
Aharbal Waterfall is a stunning waterfall on the Vishav River in Kulgam district, often called the Niagara of Kashmir, surrounded by dense pine forests perfect for picnics.
Trekking from Naranag to Gangabal Lake is a popular 2-3 day trek offering views of Harmukh peak and the pristine Gangabal and Nundkol alpine lakes.
Tulip Garden in Srinagar (Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden) is Asia's largest tulip garden, featuring over 1.5 million tulips that bloom in late March and April.
